Stop Saying "Please Find Attached"

Modern alternatives for professional email communication in 2026

January 18, 2026 · 4 min read

"Please find attached" is the email equivalent of wearing a suit from the 1990s. It's technically correct, but it screams "I learned English from a textbook." Here's how modern professionals write emails.

Why "Please Find Attached" Sounds Outdated

This phrase comes from formal business correspondence of the 20th century, when letters were physically mailed. In the digital age, it sounds:

  • Overly formal: Like you're writing to royalty
  • Passive: "Find" implies the reader has to search
  • Robotic: No one talks like this in real life

The Modern Alternatives

Outdated Phrase Modern Alternative When to Use
Please find attached... I've attached... General use, most common
Attached herewith... Here's the... Casual, friendly tone
Please see attached... Attached is... Neutral, professional
Kindly find enclosed... I'm sharing... Collaborative tone
For your perusal... For your review... Requesting feedback

Context-Specific Examples

Scenario 1: Sending a Report

❌ Outdated:
"Please find attached the Q4 report for your perusal."

✅ Modern:
"I've attached the Q4 report. Let me know if you have any questions about the revenue projections on page 3."

Why it's better: Specific, actionable, and guides the reader to key information.

Scenario 2: Sharing Code/Documents

❌ Outdated:
"Kindly find attached the updated codebase."

✅ Modern:
"Here's the updated codebase with the bug fixes we discussed. I've highlighted the changes in the README."

Why it's better: Conversational and provides context.

Scenario 3: Multiple Attachments

❌ Outdated:
"Please find attached three documents for your review."

✅ Modern:
"I've attached three files:
1. Design mockups (v2)
2. User research findings
3. Proposed timeline

The mockups incorporate the feedback from last week's meeting."

Why it's better: Organized, clear, and shows you're thoughtful.

Other Outdated Email Phrases to Avoid

Stop Saying Start Saying
As per our conversation... Following up on our call...
I hope this email finds you well Hope you're doing well
At your earliest convenience When you get a chance
Pursuant to... Following...
Please do not hesitate to contact me Feel free to reach out

The Golden Rule: Write Like You Talk

The best email writing sounds like a professional conversation. Ask yourself: "Would I say this out loud in a meeting?" If not, rephrase it.

Quick Test

Read your email out loud. If you stumble or it sounds unnatural, simplify it.

When Formality IS Appropriate

There are still situations where traditional formal language is expected:

  • Legal correspondence
  • First contact with C-level executives you don't know
  • Official company announcements
  • Regulatory or compliance communications

In these cases, "Please find attached" is still acceptable—but even then, "I've attached" works just fine.
