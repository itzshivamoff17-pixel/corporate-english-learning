Stop Saying "Please Find Attached"

Your emails sound like a 1990s fax machine. Here is how to fix them.

January 18, 2026 · 4 min read

We have all written it. "Please find attached the report." It is the zombie of corporate speak—dead, but it keeps walking.

In the modern tech world (Slack, Teams, Async), this kind of "Office Speak" signals that you are formal, distant, and perhaps a bit outdated. Modern leaders write like they speak: clear, direct, and human.

Why "Please Find Attached" Must Die

It's redundant. If there is an attachment, the recipient can see the paperclip icon. You don't need to announce it like a royal decree.

The "Action-First" Principle

Don't focus on the file. Focus on what is in the file or what the recipient should do with it.

Alternatives for Every Scenario

1. Sending a Report / Data

Instead of pointing to the file, point to the outcome.

❌ The Zombie:

"Please find attached the Q3 performance metrics for your review."
✅ The Modern:

"I've attached the Q3 metrics. Key takeaway: latency dropped by 20% in September."

2. Sending a Contract / Resume

Keep it conversational. Imagine you are handing them a physical paper.

❌ The Zombie:

"Attached herewith is my resume for your consideration."
✅ The Modern:

"Here is my resume. I'd love to discuss how my background in distributed systems fits the role."

Other Phrases to Delete from Your Vocabulary

While we are cleaning house, let's remove these other stiff phrases.

  • "As per my last email"
    Try: "Re-surfacing this to make sure it didn't get buried." (Less passive-aggressive).
  • "To whom it may concern"
    Try: "Hi [Team Name] Team," or "Hi Hiring Manager,".
  • "I trust this email finds you well"
    Try: Just start! Or simple "Hi [Name]," followed by the point.

Key Takeaways Checklist

  • Use "Here is..." or "I've attached..." instead.
  • Highlight the content/insight of the attachment immediately.
  • Write like you speak. If you wouldn't say "Herewith" in person, don't type it.
