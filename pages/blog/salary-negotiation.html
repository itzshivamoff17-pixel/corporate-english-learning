Negotiating Your Next Tech Salary Hike

The exact phrases to use when discussing compensation with confidence

January 18, 2026 Â· 6 min read

Negotiating your salary is the highest ROI activity you can do in your career. A 10-minute conversation can net you $10,000+ per year. Yet, 60% of engineers accept the first offer. Why? Fear.

This guide replaces fear with a framework. We will use principles from Harvard Law School's Program on Negotiation and psychological tactics to get you paid what you are worth.

Step 1: Mindset (Fear Setting)

Tim Ferriss, author of The 4-Hour Workweek, suggests a technique called "Fear Setting". Write down the worst possible outcome of negotiating.

The Myth (What we fear)

"If I ask for more money, they will get angry and RESCIND the offer immediately."

The Reality

Recruiting is expensive ($30k+ per hire). They have spent weeks interviewing you. They want you to sign. The worst that usually happens is they say "Sorry, we can't budge."

Step 2: Know Your BATNA

BATNA stands for Best Alternative to a Negotiated Agreement. This is your leverage.

  • Strong BATNA: "I have another offer from Microsoft for $200k."
  • Weak BATNA: "I am unemployed and have no other leads."

Rule: Never enter a negotiation without knowing your BATNA. If your BATNA is weak, focus on "Likability" rather than "Ultimatums."

Step 3: The "Total Compensation" (TC) Breakdown

Novices look at Base Salary. Pros look at TC. Tech compensation has 4 pillars:

1. Base Salary

Guaranteed cash. Focus here first.

2. Equity / RSUs

Stock grants. High risk, high reward.

3. Sign-on Bonus

One-time cash. Easiest for them to give.

4. Benefits

PTO, 401k, Remote policy.

Step 4: The Counter-Offer Scripts

The moment you get the number, pause. Say: "Thank you. I need a day to process the full package." Then, send one of these emails.

Scenario A: You have a Higher Offer (Leverage)

Subject: Questions regarding the offer

Hi [Recruiter Name],

I want to thank you again for the offer. I am incredibly excited about the team and the mission at [Company A].

As I mentioned, I am currently in the final stages with another company. They have just extended an offer with a base salary of $165k.

[Company A] is my top choice because of [Specific Reason]. If you can match the $165k base, I would be thrilled to sign the offer letter immediately and withdraw from the other process.

Best,
[Your Name]

Scenario B: You have No Other Offer (Market Research)

Hi [Recruiter Name],

Thank you for sending over the details. I'm really excited about the opportunity.

However, after reviewing the numbers, I noticed the base salary of $140k is lower than the market rate for Senior Engineers with my experience in [React/AWS], which typically averages around $160k in [City].

I am very keen to join the team. Is there any flexibility to bring the base closer to $160k?

Best,
[Your Name]

Step 5: The "Silence" Tactic

After you send that email or say the number on the phone, SHUT UP.
Silence creates pressure. In a phone negotiation, the first person to speak after a number is dropped usually loses.
Wait. Count to 10 if you have to.

Key Takeaways Checklist

  • Fear Setting: Realize they won't rescind the offer.
  • Don't anchor first: Let them give the number.
  • Use LEVERAGE: Either another offer or market data.
  • Use SILENCE: Don't babble when nervous.
