Negotiating Your Tech Salary Hike

The exact phrases to use when discussing compensation with confidence.

January 18, 2026 Â· 7 min read

A 5-minute conversation can change your financial future by $50,000+. Yet, 60% of people don't negotiate their salary because they fear appearing "greedy" or having the offer rescinded.

In tech, negotiation is expected. If you don't do it, recruiters often assume you don't know your market value. Here is how to do it professionally.

Phase 1: delaying the Number

Recruiters will ask for your "expectations" early to see if you are affordable. Don't give a number first.

The "Delay" Script

Recruiter: "What are your salary expectations?"

"Right now, I'm focused on finding the right role. I'm sure your company pays competitive market rates for someone with my experience in [Skill X] and [Skill Y]. Can we revisit the number once we determine if I'm a good fit for the team?"

Phase 2: The Counter-Offer

You got the offer! But it's lower than you wanted. Do NOT say "Is that the best you can do?" (Weak). Instead, use data.

Situation: They experienced $120k. You want $140k.

"Thank you so much for the offer. I'm really excited about the team and the mission.

However, looking at the market data for Senior Node.js Developers in this city, and considering my 5 years of experience, I was expecting something closer to the $140k range.

If we can get closer to that number, I'd be ready to sign immediately."

Why this works:

  • It shows gratitude ("Excited").
  • It references objective data ("Market data").
  • It gives a "Close condition" ("Ready to sign immediately").

Phase 3: When They Say "No" (The Pivot)

Sometimes the budget is truly fixed. This is where you pivot to the "Total Compensation Package."

"I understand the budget constraints on the base salary. Since that number is fixed, could we look at other levers? Perhaps a sign-on bonus to bridge the gap for the first year, or additional RSU grants?"

Negotiable "Levers" in Tech

  • Sign-on Bonus: One-time cash (easiest for them to give).
  • Relocation Stipend: Even if remote!
  • Start Date: More time off between jobs.
  • Equity/Stock: Often has more flexibility than salary.

Key Takeaways Checklist

  • Likability is your biggest asset. Be polite, not aggressive.
  • Always have a BATNA (Best Alternative to a Negotiated Agreement).
  • Mention "Market Value", not your personal financial needs (rent/loans).
