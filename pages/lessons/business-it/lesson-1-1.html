The 30-Second Elevator Pitch for Engineers

An elevator pitch is a brief, persuasive speech that you use to spark interest in what you do. For an engineer in an MNC, this isn't just about your job title; it's about the impact you create.

Why is it Important?

In a multinational corporation, you'll often meet senior leaders or cross-functional partners in casual settings (Zoom breakout rooms, cafeteria, or corporate events). Being able to articulate your value clearly in 30 seconds builds your professional brand.

‚ùå The Generic Way (Weak)

"I am a software engineer at Google. I work in the backend team and I use Java."

Issue: This is just a list of facts. It doesn't show value.

‚úÖ The ProSpeak Way (Strong)

"I'm a Senior Backend Engineer at Google, focusing on optimizing our payment gateway legacy code. Recently, my team reduced transaction latency by 15%, which directly improved the checkout experience for millions of users."

Why it works: It mentions Role + Specific Focus + Impact + Goal.

The 4-Step Formula

  • The Who: Your name and current title.
  • The What: The specific problem you solve or system you own.
  • The Wow: A recent achievement or metric.
  • The Why: Why you're passionate about this area.
