Mastering the Technical Demo

Live coding is the most dangerous form of public speaking. Things will break. This lesson covers the "Demo Gods" protocol and how to handle failure with grace.

The "Demo Gods" Pre-Flight Checklist

Pilots use checklists to avoid crashing planes. Engineers should use them to avoid crashing demos.

Clean Environment: Close all Chrome tabs, Slack, and Discord. Use a fresh "Demo" browser profile.
Resolution & Font Size: Set screen resolution to 1080p (easier for streaming). Increase IDE font size to 18px or 24px.
Do Not Disturb: Turn on OS-level focus mode. A text from your mom popping up during a board meeting is unprofessional.
Localhost Fallback: Have the app running on `localhost` AND a staging URL. If one fails, switch instantly.

The "Narrated Action" Technique

Silence is deadly during a demo. Never move your mouse without explaining why. Use these linking phrases:

"I'm going to navigate to the settings panel..."
"Now, I'll trigger the build pipeline..."
"While this is loading, let me explain the architecture..."

The "Backup Video" Strategy (Cheating Professionally)

Top presenters at Apple and Google often use pre-recorded videos that look like live demos. This removes all risk.

The Protocol:

  1. Record your demo perfectly using QuickTime or OBS.
  2. Keep the video file open in full screen on a separate desktop space.
  3. If the live code breaks, swipe over and say: "The network seems a bit slow, so here's a recording I made earlier to respect your time."

Remote Presentation Specifics

When presenting on Zoom/Teams, you are a TV broadcaster. Setup matters.

  • Eye Contact: Look at the camera lens, not the faces on the screen. Stick a post-it note with an arrow near your webcam.
  • Lighting: Light should be in front of you. Do not sit with a window behind you (silhouetting).
  • Audio: A $50 USB microphone sounds 10x better than built-in laptop audio. Audio quality correlates with perceived competence.

Handling Failure (When it Breaks)

If it breaks, do NOT say "It worked on my machine." Use these phrases:

❌ Don't Say

"Uh oh. That's weird. Why is it doing that? Sorry guys."

✅ Say This

"It seems we hit an edge case with the staging environment. I'll note that for the logs. Let's move to the next feature which demonstrates..."
