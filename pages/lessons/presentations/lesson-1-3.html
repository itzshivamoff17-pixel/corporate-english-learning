Visual Hierarchy for Tech Slides

Engineers often cram too much code or data onto a single slide. Visual hierarchy ensures your audience looks where you want them to look.

The 10-20-30 Rule (Modified for Tech)

  • 10 Slides Max: Keep it concise. Stakeholders have short attention spans.
  • 20 Minutes Max: Leave at least 10 minutes for Q&A.
  • 30 Point Font: If they can't read it from the back of the room (or on a small Zoom window), it shouldn't be there.

Highlighting Code Snippets

If you must show code, never show a whole file. Use "Spotlighting".

Linguistic Tip: "On line 42, you'll see the core optimization logic. Everything else on this slide is just boilerplate for context."
