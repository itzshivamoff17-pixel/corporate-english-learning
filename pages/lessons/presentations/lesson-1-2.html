The 'Hook' Meta & Live Demos

A "Hook" isn't a joke or a fancy slide. In a technical context, a hook is a Shared Pain Point. It creates a reason for the audience to pay attention.

Creating the Technical Hook

Method 1: The 'Cost' Hook

"By the end of this demo, I’ll show you how we can save $5,000/month by optimizing our cloud instances."

Method 2: The 'Error' Hook

"We’ve all seen the [X] error in our logs. Today, I'm presenting the permanent fix."

The Golden Rules of Live Demos

Demos are the ultimate proof of work. But they are also high-risk. Follow these linguistic rules to keep the audience engaged even when things go slow.

  • Narrate the Action: "As you can see, I am now initializing the script..."
  • Fill the 'Wait': "While this build completes (it usually takes 30s), I want to highlight the [X] configuration."
  • Handle the 'Fail': "It seems we have a connection timeout. No worries, I had a fallback environment ready for exactly this reason."
