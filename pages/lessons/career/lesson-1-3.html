The Language of Salary Negotiation

Negotiation is not a battle; it’s a search for a mutually beneficial agreement. The key is to shift the conversation from "What I want" to "What the market value is".

Rule 1: Never Give the First Number

If a recruiter asks for your expectations early, use the **Flexibility Pivot**.

Pivot Phrasing: "Right now, I'm more focused on finding the right fit for my technical skills. I’m happy to discuss compensation once we see that the role is a good match."

Rule 2: Negotiating the Offer

When you receive an offer, don't say "No". Say **"Is there room for...?"** or **"Based on my research..."**.

  • "Thank you for the offer! I'm very excited about the team. Based on the responsibilities and my experience in [X], I was expecting something closer to [Range]."
  • "Is there any flexibility on the sign-on bonus or the equity component?"

The 'Levers' of Negotiation

If they can't increase the Base Salary, ask for other levers: Remote work flexibility, Learning budget, or a 6-month performance review for a salary adjustment.
