Negotiation - Lesson 3

The Science of Salary Negotiation

Negotiation is not a battle; it’s a search for a mutually beneficial agreement. The key is to shift the conversation from "What I want" to "What the market value is".

Core Concept: BATNA (Harvard Law)

BATNA stands for Best Alternative to a Negotiated Agreement. Coined by Roger Fisher and William Ury in their classic book Getting to Yes, knowing your BATNA gives you power.

Calculating Your BATNA

Before you enter the call, ask yourself: "What will I do if this negotiation fails?"

Strong BATNA

"I have another offer from Company B for $150k."

Result: You can negotiate aggressively because you have a safety net.

Weak BATNA

"I have no other offers and my rent is due."

Result: Your negotiating power is low. Focus on enthusiasm and potential value.

Reference: Fisher, R., & Ury, W. (1981). Getting to Yes. Penguin.

Rule 1: Never Give the First Number

If a recruiter asks for your expectations early, use the **Flexibility Pivot**. The person who speaks first sets the "Anchor," which can limit the upper bound of the offer.

Pivot Phrasing: "Right now, I'm more focused on finding the right fit for my technical skills. I'm sure your company offers competitive market rates for senior engineers. I’m happy to discuss compensation once we see that the role is a good match."

Rule 2: Negotiating the Offer (The "Likability" Factor)

Deepak Malhotra, Professor at Harvard Business School, emphasizes that how you negotiate matters as much as what you negotiate. You want them to want to hire you more after the negotiation, not less.

The "Interest-Based" Script

Don't just demand more money. Explain why it justifies their investment.

"I am incredibly excited about the opportunity to lead the migration team.

However, the current offer of $X is lower than the industry standard for Principal Engineers with 8 years of distributed systems experience. based on my research and other conversations, I was expecting something closer to the $Y range.

I really want to make this work—is there any flexibility on the base salary or the equity component to bridge this gap?"

The Multiple Levers Strategy

If they get stuck on Base Salary, pull other levers. Negotiate the package, not just the paycheck.

  • Equity / RSUs

    "Since cash is tight, can we increase the stock grant? I believe in the company's long-term future."

  • Sign-on Bonus

    "Can we add a one-time signing bonus to off-set the equity I'm walking away from at my current job?"

  • Time & Growth

    "Can we include a guaranteed performance review at the 6-month mark?"
