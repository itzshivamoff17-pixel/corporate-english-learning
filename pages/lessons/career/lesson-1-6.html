Compensation - Lesson 6

Decoding the Benefits Package

Your "Base Salary" is only 60-70% of your total compensation (TC). In the tech world, equity and benefits can make a massive difference. You need to understand the terminology to value the offer correctly.

Equity & Stock (The "Golden Handcuffs")

ESOP vs. RSU

ESOP (Employee Stock Option Plan): The right to buy shares at a fixed price (Strike Price) later. Common in startups. Risky.

RSU (Restricted Stock Unit): Actual shares given to you. They have real cash value immediately upon vesting. Common in public companies (FAANG).

Vesting & The "Cliff"

Vesting Schedule: When you actually own the stock. A standard schedule is "4-year vesting with a 1-year cliff".

The Cliff: You get 0 shares for the first 12 months. On day 366, you get 25% of your total grant at once. If you leave in month 11, you get nothing.

Retirement & Health

401(k) Match (USA) / PF (India) / Super (Aus)

This is free money. If a company offers a "5% match", it means if you contribute 5% of your salary to retirement, they add another 5% on top. Always maximize this.

Soft Benefits (Quality of Life)

These are often negotiable even if salary is not.

  • Remote/Hybrid Stipend: $500-$1000 for home office setup.
  • Learning Budget: $1000+/year for conferences or courses.
  • PTO (Paid Time Off): "Unlimited PTO" is a trap unless the culture supports it. Ask "What is the average days off taken by the team?"

Questions to Ask HR

Before signing, send this email:

"Could you clarify the vesting schedule for the equity grant? Is it a standard 4-year monthly vest with a 1-year cliff, or is it back-weighted?"
