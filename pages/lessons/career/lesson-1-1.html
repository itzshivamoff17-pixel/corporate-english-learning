Module 1.1

Tech-Focused Power Verbs

Your resume is often the only thing between you and an interview at a top MNC. Generic verbs like "worked on" or "responsible for" do not grab attention.

Upgrade Your Vocabulary

Replace weak verbs with impact-driven ones. This helps you pass both the ATS (Applicant Tracking System) and the human recruiter's 6-second scan.

Instead of... Use...
Worked on Engineered / Architected
Helped with Collaborated / Facilitated
Improved Optimized / Refactored
Managed Spearheaded / Orchestrated

The Science of Resume Writing

Harvard Business School research reveals recruiters spend 7.4 seconds on initial resume screening. Wharton's study shows 75% of resumes are rejected by ATS algorithms before humans see them.

Google's "X by Y by Z" Formula

Accomplished [X] as measured by [Y] by doing [Z]

Example: "Reduced page load time by 40% (X) as measured by Lighthouse scores (Y) by implementing lazy loading and CDN optimization (Z)"

Reference: Bock, L. (2015). Work Rules! Twelve.

Power Verb Hierarchy

Tier 1: Leadership (Highest Value)

Spearheaded, Architected, Pioneered, Orchestrated, Championed, Transformed, Scaled

Avoid: Support Verbs (Lower Value)

Helped, Assisted, Supported, Participated, Worked on
