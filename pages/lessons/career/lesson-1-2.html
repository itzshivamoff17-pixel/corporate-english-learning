The STAR Method for Technical Scenarios

Behavioral interviews at companies like Amazon, Google, and Meta center around "Situation-based questions." The STAR method is your architectural blueprint for answering these.

Situation

Set the scene. "During the migration of our legacy auth service..."

Task

What needed to be done? "I was tasked with ensuring zero downtime while shifting 10M users."

Action

What did YOU do? (Use 'I', not 'We'). "I implemented a dual-write bridge and monitored the consistency logs."

Result

The quantifiable impact. "The migration finished 2 days early with zero data loss."

Pro Tip: Don't Forget the 'Learning'

At senior levels, recruiters often ask about failures. The "R" in STAR should then focus on the **Reflective Learning**.

"The result was a delayed release, but the learning was that we needed more robust integration tests, which I then spearheaded for the next project."
