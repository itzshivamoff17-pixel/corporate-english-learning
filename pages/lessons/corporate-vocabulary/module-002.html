Essential Meeting Phrases

Master the phrases that make you sound confident and professional in meetings.

"Let's circle back on this"

Meaning: Let's return to discuss this topic later.

When to use: When a discussion is taking too long or going off-topic.

Example: "This is an important point, but we're running short on time. Let's circle back on this in tomorrow's sync."

"Can we take this offline?"

Meaning: Let's discuss this outside of this meeting (1-on-1 or smaller group).

When to use: When a topic is too detailed or only relevant to a few people.

Example: "The database schema details are getting complex. Can we take this offline with just the backend team?"

"Just to level-set..."

Meaning: To make sure everyone has the same understanding or context.

When to use: Before diving into details, to establish common ground.

Example: "Just to level-set, we're currently at 95% test coverage, and the goal is 98% by next sprint."

"What's the action item here?"

Meaning: What specific task needs to be done, and who will do it?

When to use: To move from discussion to concrete next steps.

Example: "We've discussed the issue for 10 minutes. What's the action item here? Who's owning the fix?"

"I want to make sure we're aligned"

Meaning: I want to confirm we all agree or understand the same thing.

When to use: Before making a decision or moving forward.

Example: "Before we commit to this approach, I want to make sure we're aligned on the trade-offs."

"Can you walk me through...?"

Meaning: Can you explain this step-by-step?

When to use: When you need clarification on a process or decision.

Example: "Can you walk me through how the authentication flow works in the new system?"

"Let's park that for now"

Meaning: Let's set this aside temporarily and come back to it later.

When to use: When a topic is derailing the meeting.

Example: "The UI color scheme is important, but let's park that for now and focus on the API design."

"I'll take that as an action item"

Meaning: I will be responsible for completing this task.

When to use: To volunteer for a task or clarify ownership.

Example: "I'll take that as an action item and send the updated doc by Friday."

"Can we timebox this discussion?"

Meaning: Can we set a time limit for this topic?

When to use: When a discussion is dragging on.

Example: "Can we timebox this discussion to 5 minutes? We still have 3 more agenda items."

"What's blocking you?"

Meaning: What obstacle is preventing you from making progress?

When to use: In stand-ups or when someone seems stuck.

Example: "You mentioned the ticket is delayed. What's blocking you? Can I help remove any obstacles?"
