Presentation Openers

Grab attention in the first 30 seconds of your presentation.

Let's kick things off

Meaning: To start the meeting/presentation.

Example: "Let's kick things off by reviewing last quarter's numbers."

Thanks for joining

Meaning: Polite welcome.

Example: "Thanks for joining everyone. I know you are busy."

The purpose of this...

Meaning: Setting the agenda.

Example: "The purpose of this presentation is to align on the new roadmap."

Give you an overview

Meaning: Summarizing what will come.

Example: "I'll give you an overview of the architecture changes."

Walk you through

Meaning: To explain step-by-step.

Example: "I'm going to walk you through the user journey."

Dive right in

Meaning: To start immediately without small talk.

Example: "We have a lot to cover, so let's dive right in."

Before we begin

Meaning: Pre-conditions.

Example: "Before we begin, please mute your microphones."

Show of hands

Meaning: Asking for audience interaction.

Example: "Quick show of hands: who has used the new tool?"

Setting the stage

Meaning: Providing context.

Example: "Let me set the stage by explaining the market conditions."

Takeaway

Meaning: What you want people to remember.

Example: "My key takeaway for you today is that speed matters."
