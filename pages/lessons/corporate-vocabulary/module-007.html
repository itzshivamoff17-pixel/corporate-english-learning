Negotiation - Module 7/100

Negotiation Phrases

Negotiation isn't just about salary. It's about deadlines, scope, and resources.

Middle ground

Meaning: A compromise.

Example: "Let's find a middle ground between the design quality and the deadline."

Push back

Meaning: To resist or oppose a request.

Example: "I have to push back on this timeline; it's too aggressive."

Non-negotiable

Meaning: Something that cannot be changed.

Example: "Security compliance is non-negotiable for this release."

Trade-off

Meaning: Giving up one thing to get another.

Example: "The trade-off for faster performance is higher memory usage."

Win-win

Meaning: A situation where everyone benefits.

Example: "This partnership is a win-win for both departments."

Low-hanging fruit

Meaning: The most easily achieved of a set of tasks.

Example: "Fixing the typos is low-hanging fruit; let's do it today."

Ballpark figure

Meaning: Rough estimate.

Example: "Can you give me a ballpark figure for the cloud costs?"

Play hardball

Meaning: To act aggressively in negotiation.

Example: "The vendor is playing hardball on the license fees."

Sign off

Meaning: Official approval.

Example: "We need the CTO's sign off before we proceed."

Go to bat for

Meaning: To support or defend someone.

Example: "I will go to bat for you to get that promotion."
