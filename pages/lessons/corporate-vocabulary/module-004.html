Email Closing Phrases

Master these essential terms and phrases for professional communication.

"Please let me know if you have any questions"

Meaning: Standard polite closing

Example: I've attached the report. Please let me know if you have any questions.

"Looking forward to your feedback"

Meaning: When expecting a response

Example: I've sent the proposal. Looking forward to your feedback by Friday.

"Thanks in advance"

Meaning: Thanking before action is taken

Example: Could you review the PR? Thanks in advance.

"Let me know if this works for you"

Meaning: When proposing a time/plan

Example: I suggest we meet on Tuesday at 2 PM. Let me know if this works for you.

"Feel free to reach out"

Meaning: Inviting further communication

Example: Feel free to reach out if you need any clarification.

"I appreciate your time"

Meaning: Thanking for attention/effort

Example: I appreciate your time in reviewing this document.

"Best regards / Kind regards"

Meaning: Professional sign-off

Example: Best regards, John

"Talk soon"

Meaning: Casual, friendly closing

Example: Talk soon! Sarah

"I'll keep you posted"

Meaning: Promising updates

Example: I'll keep you posted on the deployment progress.

"Awaiting your response"

Meaning: Formal, expecting reply

Example: Awaiting your response on the budget approval.
