Email Opening Phrases

Master these essential terms and phrases for professional communication.

"I hope this email finds you well"

Meaning: A polite opening (though becoming outdated)

Example: I hope this email finds you well. I wanted to follow up on our discussion from last week.

"I'm reaching out to..."

Meaning: Modern, direct way to state your purpose

Example: I'm reaching out to discuss the API integration timeline.

"Following up on..."

Meaning: To continue a previous conversation

Example: Following up on our meeting yesterday, here are the action items.

"As per our conversation"

Meaning: Referring back to a previous discussion

Example: As per our conversation, I've attached the updated proposal.

"I wanted to touch base on..."

Meaning: To check in or discuss briefly

Example: I wanted to touch base on the deployment schedule for Q2.

"Quick question:"

Meaning: For brief, urgent queries

Example: Quick question: Do we have approval to proceed with the migration?

"I'm writing to inform you that..."

Meaning: Formal notification

Example: I'm writing to inform you that the server maintenance is scheduled for Saturday.

"Just a heads up that..."

Meaning: Informal warning or notification

Example: Just a heads up that the staging environment will be down for 2 hours.

"I wanted to loop you in on..."

Meaning: To include someone in a conversation

Example: I wanted to loop you in on the security audit findings.

"Per your request"

Meaning: In response to something asked

Example: Per your request, I've compiled the performance metrics for last quarter.
