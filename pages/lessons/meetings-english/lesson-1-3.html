Handling Interruptions Professionally

In virtual meetings, people often talk over each other. Knowing how to handle this with authority—without being rude—is a key leadership skill.

When YOU Are Interrupted

If someone cuts you off, use these phrases to regain control while maintaining the flow.

  • "Sorry, if I could just finish my thought on the database migration..."
  • "That’s a great point, let me just wrap up this section and I'd love to hear more."
  • "Hold that thought for just a second, I’m almost done with the technical overview."

When You Need to Interrupt (Interjecting)

Sometimes you must speak up to correct a technical error or clarify a point before the conversation moves on.

  • "May I interject for a moment? Regarding the load balancer config..."
  • "Sorry to jump in, but I want to make sure we’re aligned on the latency requirements."
  • "If I could add one detail to what [Name] just mentioned..."

Leadership Secret: The 'Hands Up' Hack

In Zoom or Teams, using the digital 'Raise Hand' feature is the most polite way to signal you have something to add without breaking the speaker's flow.
