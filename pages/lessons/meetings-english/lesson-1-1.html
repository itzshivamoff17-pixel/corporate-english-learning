The Daily Stand-up: Past, Present, Future

In Agile teams, the Daily Stand-up (DSU) is the most frequent speaking opportunity. Many engineers make the mistake of being too technical or too vague. The 'Past, Present, Future' framework solves this.

Phase 1: What I did (Past)

Focus on completion, not just effort.

Phrasing: "Since our last sync, I successfully pushed the fixes for..."

Phase 2: What I'm doing (Present)

Focus on your current priority.

Phrasing: "Today, my main focus is on finalizing the API integration..."

Phase 3: The Blockers (Future/Risks)

Be direct. This is your chance to ask for help.

Phrasing: "I'm currently blocked by the credentials for the staging env. Can someone help with that?"
