Technical Communication - Lesson 5

Explaining Technical Concepts to Non-Techies

Bridging the gap between code and business value using the Feynman Technique and cognitive science.

The Feynman Technique

Nobel Prize-winning physicist Richard Feynman believed that if you can't explain it simply, you don't understand it well enough. His 4-step learning method is widely used by engineers to communicate effectively.

The 4 Steps Applied to Software

  • 1. Pick the Concept: E.g., "Microservices."
  • 2. Explain it to a 5-Year-Old (ELI5): Remove all jargon.
  • 3. Identify Gaps: Where did you stumble? Where did you use buzzwords?
  • 4. Organize & Simplify: Create a compelling analogy.

Example (API):

❌ "An interface that allows multiple software components to communicate."
✅ "It's like a waiter at a restaurant. You (the user) order from the menu (the app), and the waiter (API) takes it to the kitchen (server) and brings back your food."

Reference: Gleick, J. (1993). Genius: The Life and Science of Richard Feynman. Pantheon.

Cognitive Load Theory (MIT)

Research from MIT indicates that the human brain has a limited "working memory" (about 4 chunks of information at once). Technical jargon increases extraneous cognitive load, making it impossible for stakeholders to make decisions.

The "Simplicity Audit"

Before sending an email or speaking, audit your language for these "Load Spikes":

  • Acronyms: AWS, CI/CD, K8s, JWT. (Spell them out or explain the value).
  • Abstract Concepts: "Latency," "Throughput." (Use concrete numbers: "Wait time," "Users per second").
  • Implementation Details: "We used a B-tree index." (Stakeholders don't care. Say: "We optimized the search speed").

Reference: Sweller, J. (1988). "Cognitive Load During Problem Solving: Effects on Learning." Cognitive Science.

The "Why-How-What" Framework (Simon Sinek)

Engineers often start with the "What" (the code). Business leaders care about the "Why" (the value). Always invert your explanation.

❌ The Stack-First Approach

"We are upgrading from Angular 12 to 16 because of the new hydration features and signals API..."

(CEO: "So what? Will it cost more?")

✅ The Value-First Approach

"Why: The site is currently too slow on mobile phones, losing us customers.
How: By upgrading our frontend framework.
What: We're moving to Angular 16."

(CEO: "Approved.")

Mastering Analogies

A good analogy maps a known concept (Source) to an unknown concept (Target). Here are 3 battle-tested metaphors for IT pros:

Technical Concept The Metaphor The Explanation
Technical Debt Financial Debt/Credit Card "We took a shortcut (loan) to launch fast. Now we have to pay interest (bugs/slowness) until we pay off the principal."
Scaling/Load Balancing Supermarket Checkouts "Imagine a supermarket with one cashier. The line is long. We are opening 5 more checkout lanes so customers don't wait."
Legacy Code Renovating an Old House "We can't just change the walls; we have to be careful with the wiring inside, or the lights will go out."

Reference: Pollack, S. (2020). "Using Metaphors in Technical Communication." IEEE Professional Communication Society.
