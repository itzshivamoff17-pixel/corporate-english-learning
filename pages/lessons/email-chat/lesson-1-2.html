Tone & Pitch in Professional Text

In text communication, you lose body language and vocal inflection. This is why "Direct" can often be misinterpreted as "Aggressive". Master the art of professional softening.

Direct vs. Polished

Too Direct

"Fix this bug before EOD."

Polished

"Could you please take a look at this bug? It's a priority for the EOD release."

Using 'Hedges' to Reduce Friction

Hedges are words that make your requests sound less like a command and more like a collaboration.

  • "It seems..." (Instead of "It is")
  • "Perhaps we could..." (Instead of "We should")
  • "I was wondering if..." (Instead of "Can you")

The 'Update' Pitch

When giving a status update on a delay: "We've run into a slight complication with the [Module]. We're currently [X] and expect to be back on track by [Time]."
