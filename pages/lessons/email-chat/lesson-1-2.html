Tone & Etiquette in Slack/Teams

Instant messaging is the operating system of remote work. But because text lacks tone, "Direct" is often misinterpreted as "Aggressive". Mastery of chat etiquette is mastery of team cohesion.

The "Async-First" Philosophy

Companies like GitLab and Doist champion "Async-First" communication. The golden rule: Do not expect an immediate response.

The "No Hello" Rule

There is a famous site called nohello.net. Sending "Hi" and waiting breaks focus.

Tone Softeners (Politeness Theory)

Linguists call these "hedges." In professional chat, using slightly softer language prevents you from sounding demanding.

Too Direct

"Fix this bug before EOD."

(Sounds like an order)

Polished

"Could you please take a look at this bug? It's a priority for the EOD release."

(Sounds like a request)

Too Direct

"That won't work."

Polished

"I'm not sure that approach aligns with our scalability goals. Have we considered...?"

Thread Discipline

In Slack strategies, "Thread Hygiene" is critical to prevent channel pollution.

Rule: If a conversation involves only 2-3 people or is about a specific topic, always thread it. Do not use the main channel for back-and-forth debugging.

Phrase to enforce this: "Let's move this discussion to a thread to keep the main channel clear."

Using Emoji Professionally

Research by Buffer suggests that emojis in remote work aren't unprofessional; they are emotional cues that replace facial expressions.
